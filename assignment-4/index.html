The process of creating the Logo

By Rahul Singh, part of the coursework for HCI472 - Design Fundamentals II.

The logo design process involved brainstorming ideas, sketching initial concepts, and refining the design using Adobe Illustrator. The goal was to create a logo that displayed analytical mindset, clarity and creativity.

Iterations

I tried a couple of different types of grids—simple square grids, circular ones, and a few mixed approaches combining both. Initially, I aimed to create a logo using my initials, R and S, but due to limited experience with more advanced grid construction, I couldn’t achieve a geometrically consistent result. Eventually, by relying on basic square grids and overlaying some diagonal guides, I was able to build a cleaner, more precise form. The final logo, shown here, reflects that refined process.

Final Logo Designs

The black logo on a white background communicates simplicity and clarity with maximum contrast for clean visibility.

The white logo on a black background amplifies boldness and precision, making it ideal for dark mode interfaces.

The colored logo on a white background uses #787068 and #2f2f2f to convey a neutral, grounded tone with visual softness and approachability.

The colored logo on a black background, using #dcd4ca and #787878, brings warmth and legibility in dark settings while maintaining a refined professional feel.

Import Sizes & file type

The logo was exported and used in transparent PNG format to ensure scalability and background flexibility. I imported multiple sizes, including 16×16, 32×32, 48×48, 192×192, and a high-resolution 1080×1080 version for use across favicon, navbar icons, and presentations.
